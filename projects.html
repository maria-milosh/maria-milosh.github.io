Report to the UN Special Rapporteur on human rights in Russia: deterioration of women's rights in 2024-2025

I co-authored and presented a report to the UN Special Rapporteur on Russia, Mariana Katzarova, on the rapid deterioration of women's rights in Russia after its full-scale invasion of Ukraine. Women in Russia face systemic violence: as political prisoners; as family members and victims of men who committed domestic violence or femicide, and are now pardoned after the war and return to their victims; as individuals forced into a "reproductive mission" by the state. In occupied territories and prisons, Ukrainian women endure especially harrowing abuses. Pre-existing human rights challenges have only deepened amid the crackdown on non-governmental organizations and the growing societal tolerance for violence. War and authoritarianism normalize violence across society, while the very institutions meant to protect women are being dismantled.

I hope this report informs international attention and action, however small the step.
